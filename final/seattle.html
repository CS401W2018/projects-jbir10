About Seattle

Seattle, named after Chief Sealth of the Suquamish tribe, was settled in 1851 by the Denny Party and is located on the eastern shore of Puget Sound, offering stunning views of water, mountains, and forests. The city spans 83.9 square miles and boasts over 200 miles of combined freshwater and saltwater shoreline, perfect for outdoor activities like hiking and kayaking. Seattle's mild climate features average temperatures ranging from 37°F in winter to 77°F in summer, making it ideal for year-round exploration. Visitors can enjoy the vibrant cityscape, breathtaking views, and its rich cultural and historical heritage.
